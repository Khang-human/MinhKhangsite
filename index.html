About

World War II (WW2) was a global conflict that lasted from 1939 to 1945, involving most of the world's nations. It was primarily fought between the Allied Powers, including the United States, the Soviet Union, the United Kingdom, and China, against the Axis Powers, led by Germany, Italy, and Japan. The war began when Germany, under Adolf Hitler, invaded Poland, prompting Britain and France to declare war. Key events included the Battle of Stalingrad, the D-Day invasion, and the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. WW2 resulted in an estimated 70–85 million deaths and led to significant political and territorial changes worldwide. It also set the stage for the Cold War between the US and the Soviet Union.

The results of World War II were profound and reshaped the world in many ways. The war caused an estimated 70–85 million deaths and left many cities in ruins, with economies severely damaged. The Axis Powers, including Germany, Italy, and Japan, were defeated, leading to Germany's division into East and West. The United Nations (UN) was established in 1945 to promote global peace and cooperation. Meanwhile, tensions between the United States and the Soviet Union escalated, marking the beginning of the Cold War. The war also accelerated decolonization, as many countries in Asia and Africa sought independence from European rule. To aid recovery, the Marshall Plan helped rebuild European economies, while Japan experienced rapid industrial growth with US support. Ultimately, WW2 left the US and USSR as the world's two superpowers, shaping international relations for decades.

Soviet Union

During World War II, the Soviet Union suffered the heaviest loss of life:

  • Total deaths: ~27 million (including military and civilians).
  • Military deaths: ~8.7–10.7 million (about 32–40% of total deaths).
  • Civilian deaths: ~17–19 million (due to war, massacre, famine, forced labor).

Germany

During World War II (1939–1945), Germany (Nazi) suffered extremely heavy losses, especially in 1944–1945 when the Soviet Red Army counterattacked and the Allies attacked from the West.

  • Total deaths: ~6.6–8.8 million (including military, civilians, and Germans expelled after the war).
  • Military deaths: ~4.5–5.3 million (~60–70% of total deaths).
  • Civilian deaths: ~1.5–3.5 million (due to bombing, fighting, post-war reprisals).

United Kingdom

Damage to Britain in World War II (1939–1945)

  • Total deaths: ~450,000–500,000 (including military and civilians)
  • Military deaths: ~383,700 (including forces from colonies & Commonwealth)
  • Civilian deaths: ~67,100 (mainly due to German bombing "The Blitz" 1940–1941)
